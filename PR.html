Board Game

Puerto Rico 1897

Manage plantations, erect buildings, and ship goods from the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico. Every role you choose shapes the round for everyone at the table.

2–5 Players
90–120 Minutes
7 Roles
~12 Rounds
About the Game

A Century of Strategy

Puerto Rico 1897 is a reimagining of one of the great worker-placement classics, set on the island of Puerto Rico at the close of the nineteenth century. Players compete as landowners, balancing the expansion of their plantation estates against the construction of a colonial town — all while racing to fill the cargo holds of departing ships before their rivals do.

No two sessions play out the same. The game's role-selection system means every decision you make is also a gift — or a threat — to every other player at the table. Timing, economy, and a keen sense of what your opponents need are the engines of victory.

"The great insight of Puerto Rico is that selecting a role is an act of mutual generosity: everyone performs the chosen action, but only the selector earns the privilege. Generosity, then, is strategy in disguise."

How It Works

The Core Loop

Each round, players take turns selecting a role from a shared pool. All players execute the role's action, but the player who chose it gains an additional privilege. Roles not chosen in a round accumulate bonus doubloons — making them more tempting next time.

Phase 01
Settle & Grow
Claim plantation tiles and populate your fields with colonists. Your crops — corn, indigo, sugar, tobacco, coffee — are the raw material of every future point.
Phase 02
Build & Refine
Spend doubloons on production buildings and city structures. Small buildings grant efficiencies; large buildings provide powerful end-game scoring bonuses worth up to four victory points each.
Phase 03
Trade & Ship
Move goods to the trading house for doubloons, or load cargo ships bound for Spain for victory points. The ships have limited space — coordination and timing determine who scores and who is left on the dock.
Roles

Choose Wisely

Seven roles are available each round. Pick for yourself, but know that your opponents benefit too.

🌾
Settler
All players draft a plantation tile. The Settler may also take a quarry, reducing building costs.
Privilege: +quarry option
👷
Mayor
New colonists arrive and are distributed. The Mayor takes the first extra colonist from the supply.
Privilege: +1 colonist
🔨
Builder
All players may construct one building. The Builder pays one fewer doubloon.
Privilege: −1 cost
⚙️
Craftsman
All players produce goods from staffed plantations and buildings. The Craftsman gains one bonus good of their choice.
Privilege: +1 good
💰
Trader
Players sell one good to the trading house. The Trader earns one extra doubloon on their sale.
Privilege: +1 doubloon
⚓
Captain
Players must ship as many goods as possible. The Captain earns one additional victory point.
Privilege: +1 VP
🏦
Prospector
Only the Prospector acts — taking one doubloon from the bank. Others do nothing. A tempo move.
Privilege: +1 doubloon (only)
