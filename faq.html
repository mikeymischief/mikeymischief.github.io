Commander Stats

FAQ

How the Commander Stats tracker works — the MMR graph, tiers, badges, color win rates, and what "active" means.

Game History

Click any commander's name in the pod tables on the History page. This opens the commander's profile: an art card pulled from Scryfall, a stat bar with active MMR ranking, popularity rank, win–loss record, current MMR, avg round won, and avg mulligans, a line chart of their MMR over time, rivals, and a filtered game log showing only pods they appeared in. A tier badge appears above the commander name for active commanders only.

On the MMR chart, filled points are wins, hollow points are losses, and white points are ties (🤝). Hover any point for the exact date and session number.

When you select a commander, a Rivals row appears showing the four commanders they've shared a pod with most often. Each card shows your record against them — games played together, wins, and losses. It's sorted by most games faced, with tiebreakers for how many times you've lost to them.

Clicking a rival's card will switch the view to that commander.

🔰Debut — first time this commander has ever been played in the group.
⭐First win — the first time this commander has ever won, regardless of pilot.
🔥Winning streak — this commander has won at least 2 games in a row.
🪃Returning — this commander hasn't appeared in 20 or more games.
🙌Upset — the winner had the lowest predicted win probability in the pod (requires 5+ prior plays to qualify).
🎯Undefeated — this pilot has never lost with this commander (minimum 2 plays).

At the top of the Game History page you can enter a commander for each pilot to see estimated win odds for a hypothetical pod. The model uses each pilot's recorded win rate with that specific commander, falling back to their overall rate if there's no commander-specific history yet.

Insights

A commander is considered active if they've been played at least once in the past two years. The active pool is used as the benchmark for tier and MMR rankings in History and Insights — so your rank reflects how you compare to commanders that are actually getting played, not just the all-time historical field.

Inactive commanders still appear in the data and retain an approximate tier based on where they'd land against the current active pool. On the Colors page, inactive commander names are dimmed. The On the Shelf table flags active commanders that haven't been played in 6 or more months — a heads-up that they're at risk of going inactive.

Tiers are assigned by comparing a commander's MMR to the active pool using a z-score. A competitive, evenly-matched meta could result in most commanders sitting in B with a handful in A and C and nothing at the extremes.

Tier badges appear on active commanders in the History profile card and game log, and in the MMR table on Insights. Inactive commanders have no tier badge.

SDominating — well above the rest of the field
AStrong — clearly above average
BAverage — right in the middle of the pack
CBelow average — underperforming relative to the group
DStruggling — consistently at the bottom of the field

Commanders are ranked by total games played across all pilots, with most-recent play date as the tiebreaker. The rank on a commander's profile in History and the rank in the Popularity table on Insights are calculated with the same logic.

Commander Colors

Each row shows the combined win rate across every commander that shares that color identity — it's an aggregate across all games and all pilots, not a single commander's stat. The "WU" row covers every Azorius commander the group has ever played, for example.

Dimmed names haven't been played in the past two years and are considered inactive. The Highest Win% (Active) column on the Colors page always tries to show the best active commander first. If a color pair has no active commanders, the best all-time pick is shown but dimmed to signal it reflects older data.

The same dimming applies inside the expanded accordion rows — active commanders appear at full brightness, inactive ones at reduced opacity.

Click any row in the Colors table to expand it. Every commander the group has played under that color identity appears, sorted by win rate, with win counts, total games, and a percentage for each.

Rating system

The site uses TrueSkill, a Bayesian rating system developed by Microsoft for multiplayer games. After each game, everyone's MMR is updated based on who won and what the model predicted going in.

An upset win against higher-rated opponents moves your MMR more than an expected win. Losing to a stronger field costs less than losing to weaker players. The system accounts for pod size.
