The Format

What is Commander?

Commander (also called EDH — Elder Dragon Highlander) is Magic: The Gathering's most popular casual multiplayer format. Each player builds a 100-card singleton deck led by a legendary creature called their commander, which determines the color identity of every card in the deck.

Games are typically four-player free-for-all. Your commander starts in a special command zone and can be cast from there at any time — though it costs an extra 2 mana each time it's been sent back. Players are eliminated when they reach 0 life, take 21 commander damage from a single source, or accumulate 10 poison counters.

The format rewards political play, long games, and surprising synergies. No two commander decks are ever quite the same.

Communication Tool

Commander Brackets

Brackets are a pregame communication tool to align expectations before the game starts — not a power ranking. Each bracket describes what players can expect from the decks at the table.

1
Exhibition
Theme or idea over power. Flexible rules, thematic win conditions. Games go 9+ turns.
2
Core
Mechanically focused, low pressure. Telegraphed win conditions. Games go 8+ turns.
3
Upgraded
Strong synergy and card quality. Can disrupt opponents. Games go 6+ turns.
4
Optimized
Lethal, fast, and consistent. Explosive threats and efficient disruption. Games go 4+ turns.
5
cEDH
Built to win within the cEDH metagame. Razor-thin margins, optimized for speed and efficiency. Games can end any turn.

About This Site

The Pod

This site tracks the ongoing Commander games between Mikey, Gerf, Brian, and sometimes a fourth player — a pod that's been battling it out across hundreds of games. Match results are logged after every session, and ratings are computed using a TrueSkill-style Bayesian model that accounts for the free-for-all format better than a simple win/loss record ever could.

Head to Stats for the full standings, History to dig into individual games, Insights for meta analysis, Colors for a breakdown of color identities across the pod, or the FAQ for common questions.

Source: CMDRSTATS tab, published to web as CSV. Ratings computed via a TrueSkill-style Bayesian model, not raw win/loss.
