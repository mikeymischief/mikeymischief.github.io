About This Blog

I'm Mikey — I play Magic: The Gathering, mostly in two formats: Cube Draft and Commander. This blog is where I write about both. Expect draft recaps, cube updates, Commander session logs, and the occasional design ramble.

I love games with emergent gameplay — formats where the decisions you make at the table actually matter and where no two games play out the same way. Both Cube and Commander scratch that itch in different ways.

The Synergy Cube

The Synergy Cube is a 540-card unpowered cube I've been building and tuning for years. It started as a max-power Legacy cube and evolved into something I find a lot more interesting: an environment built around archetypes and synergies, where the power level is flat enough that theme-driven decks can actually compete with good-stuff piles.

Each color section supports multiple overlapping themes, so there's real drafting to be done — reading signals, picking up on what's open, and assembling something that fits together. It drafts best at six players, which also makes it the hardest game night to schedule. Draft recaps and cube updates are posted here when I get a session in.

You can browse the full list on CubeCobra, or read the Cube Primer for the full design philosophy and archetype breakdown.

Commander

I play Commander regularly with a steady group — Gerf, Brian, and sometimes a fourth. We've been at it for years and I've been logging every game in a spreadsheet: who played what, who won, match results, the works. At some point the data got detailed enough that I turned it into a proper site.

The Commander Stats site tracks per-commander records, TrueSkill ratings, full game history, color identity breakdowns, and meta trends across our pod. Ratings are computed from scratch on every page load using the full game history pulled live from Google Sheets — so logging a game in the sheet is all it takes to update the site.

If you want to dig in: Stats has the standings and commander ratings, History has the full game log, and Insights breaks down things like seating patterns and commander popularity over time.
